ENG. YOU'RE HIRED

**Good News again! We came First in our Hub and were also given an award for the Best Solution and Presentation!

Engineering You're Hired is a mandatory module for 2nd Year students irrespective of the degree studying. It was a great opportunity for me to collaborate with people studying various degrees. University had formed hubs which had 8 teams so I was working with 2 Civil Engineering, 1 Computer Science and 2 ACSE students. The topic of our Hub was "Making Homes Smarter"

So, we all brainstormed and came up with the concept of reducing food wastage by using technology. Our aim was to design a system to aid working families in reducing food waste, saving money and improving diet, with an aim to reduce food waste by 30% and food bills by £200 per person, per year in 6 months.

Our main design was to use a customised application on a tablet unit that would scan barcodes of the food items that we are going to store in the fridge. This would also record the expiry date of the food item being placed. We will also have an option to manually input about the product for items without a bar code. The tablet would show a full list of items that are in the fridge and highlight the food item that is going to expire soon. You would also have an option to search for a variety of recipies as it would be connected to your wi-fi or hotspot.

You can access the app even on your phone to check the items at home during shopping so that you don't spend your hard-earned money. The app would also give you recommendations if it learns that some items were not used within the expiration date for e.g. Purchase less eggs next time! This would be sent as a text message to your phone and the app would also have a section where this message can be seen. An additional feature which we also thought was to have Voice Recognition which would be helpful during the checkout which means when an item is removed from the fridge or freezer, you could use your voice for e.g. "I am checking out **item name**" which would automatically remove the item from your list.

Thus, 'I am checking out' will be the common phrase that we will use all the time during checkout and it would do the rest for you!

HMIR

Python

This stands for Human Machine Interaction and Robotics. I worked on a very interesting project with a team of 3 where we had to make a Lego robot move on its own in a maze without hitting obstacles using PID controller and also search for white light which was placed at one end of the maze using Beaconing. Our Search strategy works in such a way that we just make the robot go straight and rotate right or left unless and until it finds an obstacle or a wall. If the Left distance detected by the sensor is critically less than the set value which in this case was set to (150), then the robot would go in a reverse direction for 1 second depending on which sensor catches the low value.

For example: If the left sensor detects value as 100mm, then it would make the robot perform a reverse arc towards right by running the left wheel backwards at speed (80) and the right wheel at (5) vice-versa. If the distance is not critically low which means that if any of the sensors detect a value which is in between 240 and 400, then run the PID obstacle avoidance behaviour.

We decided that the light scan was not reliable enough to locate the brightest light source (beaconing) due to external circumstances, so instead, we relied on the subsumption consisting the search strategy, PID obstacle avoidance and reverse. In order for it to find the light we just implemented a stopping behaviour as such if the light intensity that it receives is above 27 then just stop the robot during the search. Thus, all of these are called recursively so that the robot checks, avoids obstacles and also stops when it detects the light.
